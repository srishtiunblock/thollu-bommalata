Production Design and Infrastructure

The Production Design and Infrastructure Team set out to closely study the existing systems, practices and workings of the Tholu bommalata artisans. In the spirit of co-creation, we looked to identify existing areas of design aspiration and opportunity through the zoom conversations.

Our design brief was to propose ideas that filled in certain gaps, interventions and possible additions to their existing set-up, especially the details that might go unnoticed but could offer some insight or opportunity to work with the production or even open up further possibility. We worked with three areas of the production:

Proscenium

Creating a frame around the facade. Exploring the edges of the framed narrative and boundaries of our imaginations..

Sense of ceremony through a recognisable iconic form.

Opportunity to add visual interest or branding.

Enchanted forest

Taking the magic into the audience by immersing them in shadows.

Exploring and visualising similar techniques of shadow play and introducing new ones that both performers and audience may participate in.

Creating environments that appear and disappear; adding a sense of place and setting through the shifting scenography.

Back Stage and Infrastructure

Travelling Storage Case for the Puppets.

Hanging Display System for the audience interaction with the puppets.

Knock down Retail Display systems that create some frame and sense of drama around he products
