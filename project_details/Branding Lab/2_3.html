Kathakalp


Education through Storytelling

Design process/approach

We made this game as a way of having the ability of telling a story that you (the audience) can be a part of and we made it as an educational game so that the people can know about the fabulous tradition that is a part of our culture, the Indian culture. We made the game based on the tradition that is known as Tholu Bommalata. We wanted to give attention to that tradition because it is an amazing tradition but it is also a dying tradition and we do not want such a gorgeous thing to be rid of that is so important to us and the whole of India. We want to have the ability to use this type of art to tell millions of stories.

Research

We were initially inspired by Dungeons & Dragons game mechanics and the storytelling style of the Tholu Bommalata artisans. After multiple sessions of brainstorming we designed a system that merges the two.



We asked important questions and discussed age demographics. We also talked about suitable themes, characters, morals and values. FInally, we decided to target 8-9 year olds. We chose English Literature and Moral Science classes in schools as our curriculum space.

Ideation




The table screen has been designed using a regular bed table reference. We wanted this table to be light, portable and easy to handle.



We wanted this game to be as experiential as possible. Therefore, our rough sketches began on this note. We looked at this problem from every angle including material, cloth, size, light source, portability, space, magnets, etc.



At first, we thought of having an external light source under the table. Considering the electrical hazard we finally decided to use wireless and chargeable LED profile lights attached to the table.




Then, we started discussing the storyline, rules, techniques and characters of the game. We created flowcharts and wrote mock stories.




We, then, finalised our characters, obstacles, objectives, actions and dice play.The ‘fun’ and ‘relatability’ aspect was discussed in detail. Importance was given to the educational aspect as well.


Our mock stories also went through multiple reviews to ensure its clarity and relatability. Language and tone of the narration was kept in mind while writing the story. We had to eliminate words like ‘bleed’, ‘murder’, ‘kill’ and replace them with ‘hurt’, ‘injured’, etc so as to not make the story too graphic.

Analysis

Here, we talked about some more concepts and ideas that we can add to this game such as the age group and the stories. One of our team members thought of adding lights below the screen but these lights were wired so it would have definitely been hazardous to whoever is playing the game and that is why we decided to add the wireless LED lights that are placed at only two sides where the players will not be touching those lights at all. Initially, we thought of going for the younger age groups but we thought about how the moral sciences are important now so we decided to go for the elder age groups instead.

Development




Realising the complexity of our discussions, we decided to create a guidebook that will be included in the packaged box. We finalised the topics to be covered in this book and divided them among ourselves. Later, we added topics like FAQsto clear any queries the customers may have.

We then began discussing possible names for the game and came up with KathaKalp. The name ‘KathaKalp’ is derived from the words ‘Katha’ meaning story and ‘Kalpana’ meaning imagination. Other rejected names include Chayan Krida, Kaalp Krida, Gunavat, Kaalpanam, and Kathanam


We began illustrating our table screen and its mechanics. We tried to create these as simply as possible for the ease of understanding. We also had to maintain the illustration style of the artisans.



Character design was done using a grain textured brush tool on Photoshop. We tried to maintain the look and feel of the original Tholu Bommalata illustrations. After this we began designing our guidebook and animated promotional video.

The background for the promotional video was made keeping in mind the textures of the dhoti used for the screen while performing. This would create a drawing feel with it’s rawness and ruggedness.

Parts to highlight




Kathakalp allows children to experience the art of Tholu Bommalata first hand. Their interaction with the screen is the essence of the game. Creating your own story, narrating it and painting your own puppets further brings in the true feel of Tholu Bommalata. Learning moral values through an exciting and anticipating game indulges the children and ensures sheer enjoyment and deeper understanding of the meaning of these values.

Future Roadmap

Kathakalp is currently targeted at 8-9 year olds. We hope to include more younger children as this project progresses where this game could make its way into teaching the children various things such as parts of the body, counting, reciting the alphabet etc. It is currently apt for a school curriculum but we hope to include a home curriculum as well.

We hope to design an alternative option. One that is simpler and less time consuming for parents as we understand most parents are busy working individuals. We plan on creating story prompts and templates for them.

We plan on designing more diverse characters and themes to create variety. Themes such as environmental issues, good manners, etc are on the list.
