Connecting Onself


Towards a better self


What : A 15-30 second multi-sensory clip on taking few steps towards a better self in context to the ongoing festival or occasion

How: By contemplating oneself on the festivals’ significance in our lives, hence combining mythology and its perception today

Why: To tap one’s full potential spiritually, for it will be even a beautiful world that way


Context

Indian urban people celebrate festivals in different ways, often losing in the outside fancy world and ignoring the inner self. This is more true today than ever before, with the hustle-bustle of the modern metropolis, technological advancement and wealth creation. We may make such celebrations meditative, connected to oneself by making excellence a habit, reaching greater heights and making a difference spiritually. Thus, catering to the needs of the soul in a peaceful and pious way.

Impact

  1. Embrace simple living
  2. Development of body, mind and intellect
  3. To generate love, empathy, sympathy, forgiveness and a sense of selfless service
  4. Self-sufficiency

Mission


To encourage people to imbibe and practice the old-adage values of kindness, courage, etc. narrated in mythological texts such as Ramayana and Mahabharata, especially in situations when most required.

Vision


To catalyse a sattvic lifestyle among people across the globe

Philosophy


To make Tholu Bommalata significant in people’s life not only as a source of entertainment but learning

Stakeholders


Primary: Designers
Secondary: Facilitators, Tholu Bommalata Craftspersons
Tertiary: Srishti Institute

Target Audience


Primary
  1. Demographics
    1. Urban population (living in one- or two-tier cities) across India and globe
    2. International organizations endeavoring to preserve heritage and ancient artforms. For example, UNESCO, NGOs, Tourist centres, etc.
    3. Youth, the elderly; aged 18 years above
  2. Psychographics
    1. Interested in mythology, mystic poetry, philosophy, spirituality
    2. Value education intellectuals from any religion or ethnicity
    3. People who live a quality life and believe that a person’s character needs servicing periodically just like vehicles do
    4. Their interests are rational and enhance their ways of living like reading, playing sports, meditating, listening to and watching spiritual videos, etc.
    5. They have security of food, shelter, love, self-esteem and want to reach the stage of self-actualization

Secondary
  1. Rural people, craftspersons
  2. School children to inculcate values from mythologies (Greek, roman, indian, etc.)

Tertiary
  1. Researchers, students
  2. Government
