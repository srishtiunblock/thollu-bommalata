Tholu Bommallata

Also translating to ‘dance of leather dolls’ is a traditional craft practiced in Andra Pradesh, India. The artisans make intricate, translucent leather dolls and the performance usually takes place behind a screen, the shadows being projected on the screen. The performances usually are about Hindu epics and folk tales which engage and enthrall the audiences

The Labs

Annotation Lab

The lab incharge of diving into the deep and rich traditions of this craft and helping us understand them better. The Annotation lab looked at all the aspects - be it the music, the history, the performance - and asked questions. The students created great repositories of knowledge which had been largely communicated orally till now.

Performance Lab

An exploration space to search and seek methods which could enhance a craft without harming or caging its original beauty. The students looked at how they can introduce technology to enhance the performance or help the artisans. They worked closely with the artisans to strike a good balance between tradition and modernity.

Branding Lab

A space of collaborating with the artisans to help their craft gain a wider audience. The branding lab took great care that in the process of creating the brand, the original core and essence of the art is not hampered or lost. The students also worked on the social media, home decor, games and other merch
